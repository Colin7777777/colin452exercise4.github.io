Shenzhen Introduction

Shenzhen is located in the south of China, along the southern coast of Guangdong Province, on the east bank of the the Pearl River Estuary, adjacent to Daya Bay and Dapeng Bay in the east, adjacent to the the Pearl River Estuary and Lingding Ocean in the west, connected to Hong Kong across the Shenzhen River in the south, and adjacent to Dongguan City and Huizhou City in the north. It is one of the four first tier cities in China, a national economic special zone, a national economic center city, and a national innovation city, known as the "Silicon Valley of China".

Climate in Shenzhen

The climate type of Shenzhen is subtropical monsoon climate, characterized by simultaneous rainfall and abundant precipitation. Summer is hot and rainy, while winter is relatively warm and less rainy. Shenzhen is located in the South Asian tropics, and there is basically no climatological winter. It is rare for the daily average temperature to be below 10 degrees Celsius for 10 consecutive days. The precipitation distribution in Shenzhen is relatively uniform, and the difference in precipitation between spring and summer is not significant due to the influence of monsoon advance and retreat. There is also a considerable amount of precipitation in autumn and winter. In terms of temperature, the average temperature in the hottest month in Shenzhen generally does not exceed 30 degrees Celsius, while the average temperature in the coldest month is generally not less than 10 degrees Celsius.
